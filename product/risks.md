# Risks and hypotheses to validate — iterate.guru

These are the things we're testing in customer discovery and early customers. Each could redirect product direction or kill the bet. Strategic context lives in [`vision.md`](./vision.md); customer-discovery framing lives in [`vpc-head-of-design.md`](./vpc-head-of-design.md).

## Two main hypotheses

1. **Handoff packets must hold their own value even if factories never engage in-tool.** Factory work is 1–2 stages and ~10% of files in a typical bundle — the brand's internal cycle is the other 90%. The handoff packet is the structural answer: the brand assembles a subset, publishes at a pin-code URL, the factory may comment in-tool or may reply on WhatsApp as always. The hypothesis to validate is that the **packet itself as a record** — exact subset, timestamp, recipient, version — is valuable enough that brands keep producing packets even when factories don't engage. If that turns out false (brands abandon packets the moment WhatsApp wins anyway), factory engagement becomes the biggest assumption again and the first industry shifts as described in vision's "Wedge and expansion" section. Regional split matters: Western factories sometimes pull the brand into the tool (research surfaced at least one "this is the only tech pack our manufacturer will accept" quote); Tirupur / Turkish *fason* / Brazilian *facção* networks won't move off WhatsApp at all.

2. **Brands with no tool yet, not brands switching from a competitor.** The realistic early-customer profile is brands currently on Drive + spreadsheets + WhatsApp — not Backbone or Onbrand customers looking to switch. Switching from a PLM with two seasons of history, templates, and integrations is hard; the messaging, sales motion, and onboarding flow must be built for the no-tool-yet group ("you've been avoiding PLM, here's why this is different") not for switchers ("Backbone, but cheaper"). Also explicitly out of who-we-sell-to: brands that **defend** their Excel + WhatsApp stack as optimum (well-documented in Brazil, Turkey, parts of India). These are not slow adopters; they're not our market at all.

## Three secondary risks worth naming, not blocking

a. **Unit economics on $49/seat.** $49 × 4–5 designers/brand = $200–245/month per brand. Viable solo-founder economics at ~100 paying brands ($240K–$294K ARR), but punishing for the first 18 months while we get there; runway must cover it.

b. **Backbone Lite ($99) is the immediate price reference even though the product is different.** Clear positioning language ("workflow tool, not PLM") needed in every surface to avoid being categorized as "Backbone but worse-known."

c. **Migration friction.** 1 hour to first style imported, but 4–8 weeks to retire the shadow Excel a brand keeps running in parallel. Per Beyond PLM analyst consensus, data-first migration is the canonical failure pattern — onboarding must prioritize getting one active style into a bundle on day one; full data completeness comes weeks later, and the messaging must say so out loud.
